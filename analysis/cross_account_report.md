## 1. Shared strengths across all three accounts

### The emotional transformation arc
Both founders' best content follows an identical emotional structure:

1. **The unexpected starting feeling** — *"I never thought I'd feel peace next to a tiger shark"* / *"The first solo trip is never just a trip"*
2. **The tension or fear** — the world's danger, the fear of the deep, the weight of moving abroad
3. **The shift** — the moment something changes
4. **The universal insight** — a closing line that transcends the personal story and speaks to anyone

This arc appears in Rafa's three top-performing posts and in Sofia's best reel. When either founder writes this way, engagement multiplies. When they don't (minimal captions, pure aesthetics), they get far less traction.

**Avalon's opportunity:** Build a content library of destination-specific narrative Reels using exactly this arc — but centered on the places Avalon curates (Maldives, Colombia, Brazil, Türkiye, Sri Lanka), not on the founders' personal life stories.

### Latin American identity as a differentiator
- **Rafa** — Brazilian, uses Portuguese words, defends Latin American culture, strong "saudade" emotional palette
- **Sofia** — Colombian/Latin American, Providencia and Cartagena are recurring destinations, posts in Spanish, deep connection to the Caribbean and Colombia

This isn't marketing — it's who they actually are. A luxury travel brand founded by Latin Americans who genuinely love and travel Latin America is a far more credible guide to Cartagena or Providencia than a generic agency. This authentic authority needs to be stated explicitly and shown repeatedly.

### Ocean and adventure as the core identity
- Sofia: professional-level diver, shark encounters, freediving, multiple Fuvahmulah trips — this is her life
- Rafa: travel as self-liberation, freedom through movement
- Avalon: *"ocean lovers first, travel curators second"*

The ocean-adventure differentiator is not manufactured positioning — both founders actually live it. This makes it defensible, credible, and deeply authentic. No generic luxury travel agency can compete with founders who actually free-dive with tiger sharks in the Maldives.

### Personal warmth over corporate distance
All three accounts feel like real people, not brands. Even Avalon's brand posts (their original 4) use warm, first-person language — *"We are building Avalon with care"*, *"a genuine love for discovering the world."* This warmth is the brand's most valuable intangible asset and must be protected at every content decision.

## 3. What the brand account is missing that the founders do well

### Narrative depth in videos
Avalon's best-performing videos are all cross-posted from the founders. Avalon's own original videos (World Ocean Day) have a fraction of the performance. The difference is narrative — the founders write scripts, Avalon writes announcements. Avalon needs to write scripts.

### Specificity
Rafa's viral captions are specific: *"At first, it feels scary. Then it feels quiet. Then it feels like freedom."* Three precise emotional stages. Avalon's original posts are general: *"personal, meaningful, and unforgettable."* Every travel brand says that. Specificity is what creates resonance.

### A personal subject at the center
Both founders' best content features themselves — a feeling they had, a fear they overcame, a realization they reached. Avalon's original posts are about the brand, not about a person. The founder introduction post (April 9) is the exception — and it got 3x more comments than any other original post.

**Sofia's unique strength:**

**Avalon's emerging strength:**
The brand positioning (*"ocean lovers first, travel curators second"*) is the most differentiated thing about the Avalon account. If this line becomes a repeated brand truth — backed by Sofia's actual dives and specific destination content — it could become the defining sentence of the brand.

## 6. Recommended brand voice for Avalon Escapes

Based on what's resonating across all three accounts, Avalon's content voice should be:

**Structure:** Rafa's narrative stanza format (short parallel lines, emotional arc from tension to transformation)

**Subjects:** Sofia's destinations and ocean expertise (specific places, specific experiences, specific feelings)

**Tone:** Neither Rafa's philosophical introspection nor Sofia's minimalist aesthetics alone — a blend that is **specific, warm, transformative, and rooted in real experience**

**Language:** Bilingual (English and Spanish) where natural — never forced translation, but authentic bilingualism that reflects the founders

**What Avalon should never sound like:**
- *"luxury escape in paradise"* — generic
- *"tailor-made experiences designed for you"* — every agency says this
- *"where memories are made"* — meaningless
- Any caption that could have been written by an AI about any travel destination

**What Avalon should always sound like:**
- Someone who actually swam with tiger sharks in Fuvahmulah talking about what that feels like
- Someone who knows the difference between Providencia and Cartagena because they've been to both, not because they read a listicle
- A friend who happens to be an expert, not an agency

## 8. Priority recommendation — the one thing Avalon should do immediately

**Start posting original narrative Reels from the founders about specific Avalon destinations — at least once per week.**

The cross-posting strategy has given Avalon almost all of its reach so far, but cross-posted content builds Rafa and Sofia's personal brands, not the Avalon brand. The account needs content that is:
- Explicitly about a destination Avalon curates
- Narrated in the founders' authentic voices
- Using the emotional arc that has already proven to work
- With "Avalon Escapes" mentioned naturally, not pushed

One such Reel per week — even just Sofia narrating a 30-second clip about what freediving in Fuvahmulah felt like, with the Avalon handle — would start building the brand's own content identity. At the current pace of 4 original posts in 63 days, the account won't build a real audience.

The data shows the founders are capable of producing viral content. The only thing missing is pointing that capability at the Avalon brand.
