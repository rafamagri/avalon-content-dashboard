## Classification Tiers

Every pattern, hook, script, and caption formula in this report carries one of three labels.

**TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation**
The format is already trend-based. Recreate it directly with Avalon's own footage, voice, destinations, and brand angle. No transformation required beyond filling in Avalon's content.
*Examples: POV formats, "send this to" formats, list Reels, keyword CTAs, invitation formats, transition trends, travel reminder formats, flag + emotion declarations.*

**TIER 2 — Structural Replication**
Replicate the structure — hook formula, emotional arc, pacing, caption shape, CTA style — but not the exact words, footage, creator identity, or personal story. Transform the skeleton into Avalon's own content.
*Examples: expectation-flip formula, philosophical essay arc, curiosity-gap hook pattern, milestone narrative structure.*

**TIER 3 — Inspiration Only**
Do not copy directly. The content is too tied to a specific creator's identity, personal story, or signature voice. Use only as a directional idea — the underlying emotion or insight may transfer, but the execution must be original.
*Examples: personal manifestos, signature phrases ("That's what they call it at least…"), private experience framing, unique editing identity, deeply personal journey stories.*

### PATTERN 1 — The Curiosity-Gap Hook
`TIER 2 — Structural Replication`
**Accounts:** @colinduthie, @seanhammonds, @noareserrunt, @monicaroams, @gavinheeks

A hook that opens with just enough information to create tension but withholds the key reveal, forcing the viewer to keep watching.

**Examples from data:**
- *"That's what they call it at least…"* — @noareserrunt (53M plays, 1.6M likes — highest in the group) → **TIER 3** for this exact phrase (too signature to the creator)
- *"The city that time forgot."* — @colinduthie (Chernobyl, 1M plays)
- *"nothing like we expected"* — @colinduthie (Ethiopia)
- *"I couldn't believe my eyes when I saw all of the cobwebs"* — @seanhammonds (102M plays)
- *"still can't believe this is in South Africa 🤯"* — @monicaroams
- *"who knew this was JAPAN"* — @gavinheeks (2M plays)

**Why it works:** The brain is wired to resolve incomplete information. A hook that opens a gap creates cognitive tension that can only be resolved by watching. Shorter and more mysterious = higher pull.

**The replicable formula:** [Something surprising] + [a reason to doubt the conventional expectation]
**Adaptation note:** The curiosity-gap structure is fully replicable. The specific one-liner "That's what they call it at least…" is @noareserrunt's signature — do not use.

### FORMULA 1 — The "That's Not What I Expected" Arc
`TIER 2 — Structural Replication`
**Source:** @seanhammonds Pakistan series, @colinduthie Iran, @jackrosen6 Brasil

```
[0–3 sec]  Hook: State the misconception directly. ("You think you know [destination].")
[3–10 sec] Tension: Show something that challenges the assumption (beauty, warmth, normalcy)
[10–20 sec] Rising surprise: More unexpected moments, personal reactions
[20–35 sec] Emotional peak: The human connection moment
[35–45 sec] Resolution: "I was wrong. And I'm grateful."
[Caption]  The written version of the emotional flip, ending with a universal truth
```

**Adaptation note:** The arc is fully replicable. The specific stories (Sean in Pakistan, Colin in Iran) are personal to those creators. Avalon applies this to its own destinations — Fuvahmulah (not the postcard Maldives), Providencia (not the Colombia everyone knows), the Sacred Valley (not just Machu Picchu).

## E. Visual and Editing Patterns

### V1 — Slow Aerial Establishing Shot
`TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation`
Every cinematic travel creator in this group opens premium destination Reels with slow aerial footage. Signals scale, beauty, and production quality within the first 2 seconds. @colinduthie, @colemangeiger, @gavinheeks all use this consistently.

### V2 — First-Person POV Underwater
`TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation`
@gavinheeks' highest-performing single post is pure underwater footage (3.1M plays). The first-person ocean POV — mask, bubbles, marine life at eye level — is the most-shared visual format in ocean content. No narration required.
**Directly relevant to Avalon:** All Sofia diving footage and Fuvahmulah content should be shot in first-person POV where possible.

### V3 — Split-Screen Expectation vs. Reality
`TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation`
Show what the world told you a destination looks like vs. what it actually is. Text overlay on screen. Implied by @colinduthie and @seanhammonds's expectation subversion content.

### V4 — Text-on-Screen Parallel Lines
`TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation`
Simple white text on black background, one line at a time, appearing as narration or music plays. Minimal, literary, reads like a book. @noareserrunt's viral posts use this format. Widely used Instagram Reel style.

### V5 — Real-Time Reaction Shot
`TIER 2 — Structural Replication`
Filming your own face the moment something extraordinary happens — the awe, the shock, the laughter. @seanhammonds (cobweb/spider Bali — 102M plays), @jackrosen6 (Well of Death, India). The reaction IS the content.
**Note:** Only works if the reaction is genuine. Do not stage it.

### V6 — Morning Routine at an Extraordinary Location
`TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation`
Short "day in my life" format, starting from waking up somewhere unexpected. Coffee on a volcano. Sunrise yoga on salt flats. Breakfast with no walls. Makes viewers imagine themselves there. @colinduthie's Guatemala and Socotra morning content.

### V7 — The Static Hero Shot
`TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation`
Ultra-high-quality single establishing shot held for 3–5 seconds before any movement. Signals "I don't need to rush — this image is strong enough." Used by @colinduthie for Chernobyl, Gstaad, Yemen. Minimal editing required.

### V8 — Fast-Cut Cultural Immersion
`TIER 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation`
Rapid cuts between cultural moments — food, faces, music, motion, color. @jackrosen6's India wedding, Brazil, Egypt content. Designed to trigger FOMO. Standard travel Reel format.

## F. Avalon Adaptation Guide

The five most powerful patterns with full Avalon adaptation briefs.

---

### F1 — Curiosity-Gap Hook → Avalon Version
**CLASSIFICATION:** Tier 2 — Structural Replication

**ORIGINAL:** *"That's what they call it at least…"* / *"The city that time forgot"* / *"who knew this was JAPAN"*
**WHY IT WORKS:** Cognitive tension. The brain cannot scroll past an unresolved question.
**AVALON VERSION:** *"They call it the Maldives."* [Cut to Fuvahmulah.] *"But Fuvahmulah is something the Maldives photos never show you."*
Or: *"They told me Cartagena was just for tourists."* [Shows Providencia.] *"They were wrong."*
**WHAT TO AVOID:** Using @noareserrunt's exact phrase "That's what they call it at least…" — it is their signature. Making the mystery too vague — the hook must hint at something genuinely worth discovering.

---

### F2 — Philosophical Essay → Avalon Version
**CLASSIFICATION:** Tier 2 — Structural Replication

**ORIGINAL:** *"Society doesn't kill dreams overnight…"* (noareserrunt) / *"Home is not a place but a feeling"* (seanhammonds)
**WHY IT WORKS:** Universal insight makes every reader feel the post is addressed to them personally. Gets saved, sent to friends, shared in stories.
**AVALON VERSION:** *"They told you that spending [X] on a trip was irresponsible. / They said save it. Wait. Be realistic. / But here's what they didn't tell you: / You will spend that money on something. / The question is whether it will still mean something in 10 years. / A trip to the right place at the right time with the right people / will live in you longer than anything else you could buy. / That's what Avalon builds. Not trips. Evidence."*
**WHAT TO AVOID:** Using @noareserrunt's specific language. Being preachy — tone must be warm and personal, not lecturing. Lead with "I" not "you."

### F3 — The Invitation Reel → Avalon Version
**CLASSIFICATION:** Tier 1 — Direct Trend Adaptation

**ORIGINAL:** *"YOUR CHANCE TO JOIN US — Only 5 spots"* (gavinheeks) / *"who wants to join next time?"* (monicaroams) / @viluagency's trip announcements
**WHY IT WORKS:** Converts aspiration into a literal action. Creates urgency. Drives DMs and comments simultaneously.
**AVALON VERSION:** *"We're going back to Fuvahmulah in October. / Tiger sharks. Sunrise dives. Eight people maximum. ## G. Content Scoring System

Rate any Avalon post idea before creating it. Target 80+ before posting.

| Criterion | Max | What earns full marks | What earns zero |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook strength** | 20 | Opens with a hook formula from Section B, or a clear curiosity gap / emotional reframe. Hook is the very first line. | Generic opener ("We love the Maldives!"), passive statement, nothing that creates tension |
| **Specificity** | 15 | Names a real place, feeling, person, or sensory detail only someone who's been there would know | Any phrase applicable to any destination ("breathtaking views," "luxury escape," "unforgettable experience") |
| **Emotional arc** | 15 | Clear tension → shift → insight, or unexpected → realization → invitation. Something changes by the end. | Flat description, information dump, list without emotional progression |
| **Brand fit** | 15 | Sounds like Rafa or Sofia personally. Warm, authentic, premium, non-corporate. Could only be Avalon. | Sounds like a travel brochure, a generic influencer, or a copy of another account |
| **Shareability / save trigger** | 10 | Contains a universal insight, a surprising fact, or a clear reason to save or send to a friend | Enjoyable but forgettable — nothing to share or return to |
| **CTA quality** | 10 | Specific, keyword-driven action (DM/comment word), genuine invitation, or a question requiring a personal answer | No CTA, generic "double tap if you agree," or CTA buried at the end with no context |
| **Adaptation integrity** | 10 | Tier 1: recreated from Avalon footage. Tier 2: structure transformed, story is Avalon's own. Tier 3: content is genuinely original. | Direct copy of another creator's caption, script, or personal story regardless of tier |
| **Format match** | 5 | Uses the Reel/photo/carousel format that best fits the content | Wrong format for the content type |

**TOTAL: 100 points**

| Score | Decision |
|---|---|
| **80–100** | Post it. Ready. |
| **60–79** | Refine before posting. Identify the weak criterion and fix it specifically. |
| **40–59** | Rethink. The idea may be sound but execution needs rebuilding from the hook up. |
| **Below 40** | Discard or save for later. Low-scoring content trains the algorithm against you. |

**Quick diagnosis guide:**
- Low hook → Rewrite the first line using a hook from Section B
- Low specificity → Add one detail only someone who's actually been there would know
- Low emotional arc → Find the tension. What did you fear? What changed? What surprised you?
- Low brand fit → Rewrite in first person, in Rafa or Sofia's actual voice
- Low shareability → Add a sentence that a friend would send to another friend
- Low CTA → Add a keyword prompt: "Comment [WORD] for our guide" or "DM us [WORD] to join"
- Low adaptation integrity → Check the tier. Tier 3 content must come from a genuine Avalon story, not adapted from the reference group.
