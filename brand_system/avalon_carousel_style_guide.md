# Avalon Escapes — Carousel Style Guide

## What Makes an Avalon Carousel Work

Carousels are Avalon's highest-leverage format for saves, shares, and new-audience reach.
They work as **guides, not galleries**. The goal is to give the viewer something worth saving.

Primary reference: **@travelcroats** — study their first-slide hooks, slide flow, and save triggers.

---

## Slide Structure (Default)

| Slide | Role | Content |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Cover / Hook** | Stop the scroll. Make them swipe. | Bold headline, single strong visual, no clutter. The promise of what's inside. |
| **2 — Context** | Establish authority or scenario | Brief setup: where, what, who, why |
| **3–6 — Value slides** | Deliver the promise | Numbered tips, destinations, recommendations, itinerary beats, comparisons |
| **7–8 (optional)** | Depth / detail | The thing most guides skip — Avalon's specific insider knowledge |
| **Last — CTA** | Convert attention to action | Save trigger + comment prompt + follow CTA. One clear next step. |

---

## First-Slide Design Rules

The first slide is the only slide most people see. It must:

1. **Have a headline that sounds like a promise, not a label**
   - "The 5 hotels in the Maldives worth the money" ✓
   - "Maldives Hotels" ✗
2. **Use the biggest, boldest text in the carousel** — Montserrat Black
3. **Have a single dominant visual** — not split, not collaged
4. **Show a number or list signal** — "5 things / 3 islands / 7 days"
5. **Be readable in 2 seconds** — the test: screenshot it, shrink it to thumbnail size, still readable?

---

## Visual Style Per Slide

### Dark-overlay slides (text on photo)
- Full-bleed photography
- Dark gradient overlay (bottom 40–60%) — navy `#0F2649` at 70–80% opacity preferred
- White Montserrat text on top
- One piece of text per slide — never two competing statements

### Clean text slides (no photo)
- Background: `#0F2649` (navy) or `#213A3E` (teal)
- White Montserrat text
- Optional: single white line icon as decorative element
- Use for tip lists, numbered steps, or text-heavy guidance

### Logo placement
- Isotype (butterfly symbol) bottom-right or top-left, white, low opacity (30–40%)
- Full imagotype only on cover/first slide if used — never on body slides

---

## Typography on Slides

| Element | Font | Weight | Size guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| First slide headline | Montserrat | Black | Dominant — fill the slide |
| Slide header/number | Montserrat | Bold | Prominent but secondary to visual |
| Body text / tips | Montserrat | Medium | Comfortable reading size |
| Caption accent / location label | Montserrat | Medium spaced | Small, all-caps with dashes |

**Maximum text per body slide:** 40–60 words. If you have more to say, split the slide.

## Caption for Carousel Posts

### Structure:
```
Line 1: The hook — what this carousel delivers (mirrors slide 1 headline)
[blank line]
Lines 2–4: The personal angle — why Rafa or Sofia is the right person to share this
[blank line]
The list signal: "Save this for your next trip." or "Which one are you booking first?"
[blank line]
CTA: Comment prompt + keyword save prompt
[blank line]
Hashtags (8–15 relevant, not spammy)
```

### Caption tone:
- Personal, first-person, specific
- Never "I'll link below" or "check the bio" — those kill saves
- The caption should add value the slides don't repeat

## Save & Share Triggers

### Save triggers (use one per carousel):
- "Save this before you plan your trip."
- "Save for when you're ready to go."
- "Screenshot the last slide — it's the one thing most guides miss."
- "Save this — your future self will thank you."

### Share triggers:
- "Send this to whoever needs to see [destination]."
- "Tag someone who needs this trip."
- "Share this with the person who always plans your holidays."

### Comment triggers:
- "Which of these would you book first?"
- "Drop a 🦋 if you've been / ✈️ if you want to go."
- "What's your next escape?"

## Carousel Types (by purpose)

| Type | First Slide Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Destination Guide** | "X things to do in [Destination] you won't find on Google" | "7 experiences in Sri Lanka that will change your trip" |
| **Itinerary** | "The perfect X-day [Destination] itinerary" | "The perfect 10-day Maldives itinerary" |
| **Comparison / Decision helper** | "[Option A] vs [Option B]: which is right for you?" | "North Malé vs South Malé: where to stay" |
| **Hotels/Stays** | "The X hotels in [Destination] worth the splurge" | "The 4 boutique hotels in Kaş we'd book again" |
| **Packing / Practical** | "What we actually bring to [experience]" | "What Sofia actually brings on every freediving trip" |
| **Story / Journey** | "We spent X days in [Destination]. Here's what actually happened." | Founder storytelling arc across slides |
| **Trend Adaptation** | Adapt @travelcroats guide format with Avalon destinations | Any structured "X things" or itinerary format |

## Avalon Carousel vs Generic Travel Carousel

| Generic | Avalon |
|---|---|
| "Top 10 things to do in Maldives" | "The Maldives experiences most resorts won't tell you about" |
| Copied TripAdvisor tips | Rafa or Sofia's firsthand, specific recommendations |
| Stock photos of generic hotels | Real places Avalon has actually vetted |
| "Beautiful beaches!" | "The sandbank only accessible by private boat — and how to get there" |
| Hashtag-stuffed caption | Intentional caption with one clear save trigger |

---

## The Save Test

Before publishing any carousel, ask:
> "Would I save this if someone else posted it?"

If no: the hook isn't strong enough, the tips aren't specific enough, or the value isn't clear enough.
