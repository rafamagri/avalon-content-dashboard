## Recurring Visual Patterns

### 1. Navy-as-everything
Deep navy (`#0F2649`) is not just a background — it is the emotional atmosphere.
Used as:
- Solid background on text-only/minimal slides
- Overlay on full-bleed photography (60–70% opacity)
- The "mood" underneath every image — darkening it into something premium

The effect: every photo, no matter the destination, feels like it belongs to the same world.

### 2. Two-layer text hierarchy (THE signature visual move)
Every impactful Avalon slide uses a deliberate contrast between:
- **Montserrat Black** (large, confident, modern) — the statement
- **Amore Christmas italic** (smaller, elegant, warm) — the soul

Example: "QUÉ SIGNIFICA" in Montserrat Black → "Avalon?" in Amore Christmas italic.
This is the most recognizable and replicable Avalon visual pattern. Use it consistently.

### 3. Gold/warm amber accent system
Gold appears only on small, specific elements — never as a dominant color:
- Category labels: "CO-FOUNDER", "MITOLOGÍA CELTA"
- Tag lines: "◆ CURATED · LUXURY · TRAVEL ◆"
- Brand values: "◆ PAZ · SANACIÓN · ABUNDANCIA · CONEXIÓN ◆"
- Thin divider lines between text blocks
- Headline on "THE WORLD AWAITS" slide (the one exception to white — used for warmth)

Rule: gold is a whisper, not a statement. One or two uses per slide, maximum.

### 4. Diamond/crosshatch texture overlay
A faint geometric diamond grid appears on some slides as a texture layer over photos.
Very subtle — barely visible — adds a premium, editorial layer without competing with content.
Use sparingly. Not every slide needs it.

### 5. Butterfly isotype — always quiet
The butterfly+compass symbol appears on virtually every slide but is never large.
It is a presence, not a focal point.
- Default position: top-right or bottom-right corner
- Size: small enough to miss on first glance
- Color: white or light outline
- Never centered, never dominant

### 6. @avalon.escapes handle placement
Small white text, top-left corner on formatted slides. Consistent across the carousel set.
This is the brand attribution pattern — always top-left, always small, always there.

### 7. Thin gold divider lines
Single-pixel horizontal gold lines separate major text blocks on several slides.
They signal structure, breathing room, and editorial restraint — a luxury publishing detail.

### 8. "◆ WORD · WORD · WORD ◆" tag format
The diamond-dot tag format appears at the bottom of key slides.
Used for brand values, destination categories, and brand description.
Examples:
- "◆ CURATED · LUXURY · TRAVEL ◆"
- "◆ PAZ · SANACIÓN · ABUNDANCIA · CONEXIÓN ◆"
- "PAZ · SANACIÓN · ABUNDANCIA · CONEXIÓN"
This is a proprietary brand pattern. Use it consistently in slide footers.

### 9. Circular portrait photos for founders
Founder slides use a circular headshot photo in the top-right corner over the background photo.
Creates a human face at the top of the slide — personal, trustworthy, approachable.
Works alongside the large "MEET SOFIA / MEET RAFAELLA" headline.

### 10. Full-bleed authorial photography
Photos are not stock. They are real, earned, experiential.
Treatment: fill the slide, darken to work as background, let the photo breathe underneath text.
Crops prioritize mood over documentation — sky, water, light, scale.

### 11. Slide navigation dots
Instagram carousel dot indicators appear in Avalon slides.
Not hidden or removed — they are part of the design, centered at the bottom.
Confirms this is explicitly designed for carousel format.

### 12. "EST. 2022" brand credibility signal
Small text, bottom-right corner on some slides. Anchors the brand in time without being prominent.

## Typography Hierarchy

| Level | Font | Weight | Size | Color | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 — Headline | Montserrat | Black | Very large (fills 30–50% of slide width) | White | Section title, declaration, question |
| L2 — Tagline/Soul | Amore Christmas | Italic | Large (secondary to L1) | White | Emotional beat, brand name, defining phrase |
| L3 — Label/Category | Montserrat | Medium | Small, all-caps, tracked | Gold/amber | Role labels, categories, tags |
| L4 — Body | Montserrat | Medium/Regular | Reading size | White | Paragraph copy, descriptions, quotes |
| L5 — Footer tag | Montserrat | Medium | Very small, all-caps, tracked | Gold/white | Value tags, "EST. 2022", handle |
| L6 — Micro label | Montserrat | Medium | Tiny, spaced | Gold | "DESLIZA PARA DESCUBRIR", "NO CREAMOS VIAJES" |

**Critical rule:** Never put two L1 elements on the same slide. One headline, one soul line — that's the pattern.

## Image Treatment

| Element | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Overlay** | Navy `#0F2649` at 60–70% opacity — darkens images uniformly |
| **Crop** | Full-bleed, portrait orientation (4:5 for Instagram), fills entire slide |
| **Brightness** | Deliberately underexposed or darkened — never blown-out or overly bright |
| **Saturation** | Natural — not HDR, not over-processed, not filter-heavy |
| **Subject** | Landmarks, boats, beaches, ocean, temples, founders in context — never staged stock |
| **Collage** | Used once (founders destination proof) — 3×4 grid, tight spacing, authorial photos only |

## Carousel Pacing (observed in published carousels)

### "Qué significa Avalon?" (4-slide pacing)
1. **Intrigue** — question hook + swipe instruction
2. **History/origin** — mythological depth
3. **Emotional meaning** — personal interpretation
4. **Invitation** — CTA + "ESCRÍBENOS"

### Founders Carousel (6-slide pacing)
1. **Declaration** — punchy tagline cover
2. **Mission** — why we exist
3. **Human 1** — Meet Rafaella (personal story)
4. **Human 2** — Meet Sofia (personal story)
5. **Promise** — what we guarantee
6. **Proof** — destination photo collage

### First Post Carousel (13+ slides, 4 observed)
1. **Cover** — brand name + tagline
5. **Services** — what we offer
7. **Aspiration** — world awaits philosophy
13. **End card** — full logo + CTA

**Pattern:** Avalon carousels have clear emotional arcs, not just information lists.

## What Feels Uniquely Avalon

1. The Celtic mythology origin story — no other travel brand uses this
2. The "PAZ · SANACIÓN · ABUNDANCIA · CONEXIÓN" value sequence — poetic and specific
3. Two-language authenticity — Spanish and English, neither apologetic
4. Emotional etymology: explaining what a word/name *really* means as a brand-building device
5. The butterfly+compass isotype as a transformation metaphor, not just a logo decoration
6. Founders are visible, named, and personal — Rafaella and Sofia with their real stories
7. "Feel a place, not just visit it" — the defining differentiator line

## What Should Be Repeated

- The two-layer typography system (Montserrat Black + Amore Christmas italic)
- The navy overlay on authorial photography
- The "◆ WORD · WORD · WORD ◆" tag format at slide footer
- Gold for labels and values only (not headlines)
- Butterfly isotype whisper on every slide
- @avalon.escapes top-left handle on all formatted content
- Short, declarative 3-beat sentences for covers: "X. X. X."
- The "DESLIZA PARA DESCUBRIR" / "SWIPE" instruction on hook slides
- Thin gold divider lines between text blocks

---

## What Should Not Be Overused

- The diamond crosshatch texture — once per carousel maximum
- The full imagotype logo in slides — reserve for end cards only
- Gold as a headline color — one use in 13+ slides observed
- Collage format — powerful but rare; once per 4–6 carousels
- Long body copy — only Mission/Promise/Offer slides carry paragraphs
- Destination lists in a single slide — break into slide sequences
- The "DESLIZA PARA DESCUBRIR" instruction — hook slides only, not every slide
