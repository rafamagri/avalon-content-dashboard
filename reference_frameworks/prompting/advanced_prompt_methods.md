# Advanced Prompt Methods — Avalon Escapes

> These techniques extend any GOAL prompt and unlock deeper, more critical, more creative AI output.
> Use them when a standard prompt gives you something technically correct but not strategically sharp.

---

## 1. Devil's Advocate

### What it means
Ask the AI to actively challenge the idea before improving it. Make it argue against your content, identify weaknesses, and explain how it could fail — before presenting the better version.

### When Avalon should use it
- When a content idea feels "too safe" or obvious
- Before committing to a weekly plan
- When a caption feels flat but you can't identify why
- When the hook sounds good but hasn't been stress-tested

### Example prompt

```
[DEVIL'S ADVOCATE MODE]

Challenge this Reel concept before improving it. Tell me every reason it could fail: why the hook might not stop the scroll, why the caption might feel generic, why the CTA might not drive action. Be specific and honest. Then — having identified the weaknesses — give me the stronger version.

GOAL Prompt:
🎯 GOAL: Drive DM inquiries for our Maldives trips
📏 OBJECTIVE: A 60-second Reel with strong hook, visual arc, and keyword CTA
🧰 ASSETS: Avalon Escapes brand, Fuvahmulah diving, curiosity-gap format (Tier 2)
🖼️ LAYOUT: Hook + visual sequence + caption (110 words) + CTA
```

### How it helps content quality
- Surfaces clichés before they're posted
- Forces specificity where ideas are vague
- Prevents "good enough" from being the enemy of great
- Trains sharper thinking about what actually makes content perform

## 2. Client Lens

### What it means
Ask the AI to respond as Avalon's ideal client — not as a marketer or content creator. The AI reads your content with the specific eyes of someone who might hire Avalon, and tells you how it lands emotionally and practically.

### When Avalon should use it
- When testing whether a caption feels real or performative
- When checking if a CTA is compelling or generic
- When evaluating whether a destination guide feels trustworthy
- Before posting a founder story to check if it feels relatable or self-promotional

### Example prompt

```
[CLIENT LENS MODE]

You are Avalon Escapes' ideal client: a 35-year-old professional, dual income, values meaningful experiences over standard tourism, follows 20+ travel creators on Instagram, has the budget but hasn't found an agency they trust yet. You're scrolling Instagram.

Read this post and tell me: Does it stop your scroll? Does it make you feel something? Does it make you want to DM them? What doubts do you still have after reading it? What would push you to take action?

Post to review:
[INSERT FULL CAPTION + CTA HERE]
```

### How it helps content quality
- Reveals the gap between creator intent and audience perception
- Shows whether trust is being built or eroded
- Makes objections visible before they're hidden in silence
- Shifts frame from "what I want to say" to "what they need to hear"

## 3. Consult the Greats

### What it means
Ask the AI to evaluate your content or strategy through the lens of specific experts, brand builders, or iconic thinkers — and then synthesise their perspectives into one clear recommendation.

### When Avalon should use it
- When you need a strategic lens, not just a copywriting fix
- When evaluating a brand direction or positioning decision
- When trying to understand why a post didn't work
- When building a long-term content strategy

### Example prompt

```
[CONSULT THE GREATS MODE]

Evaluate this content direction through four different lenses:

1. David Ogilvy — the advertising and copywriting master: Is the headline clear? Is it specific? Does it earn attention honestly?
2. Rory Sutherland — the behavioural economist: What's the psychological mechanism at play? Is this triggering the right mental shortcut?
3. @noareserrunt — the most viral travel creator in our reference group (160× average performance): Is the hook strong enough? Is the emotional arc compressed enough to hold attention in 60 seconds?
4. A boutique luxury travel founder who built from scratch: Is this the content that builds a brand people trust, or just content that chases reach?

Synthesise all four into ONE clear recommendation.

Content direction to evaluate:
[DESCRIBE THE CONTENT IDEA OR STRATEGY]
```

### How it helps content quality
- Multi-perspective critique catches blind spots
- Forces you to think like a strategist, not just a creator
- Reveals when viral logic and brand logic are in conflict
- Produces insights that a single "improve this" prompt never would

## 4. Back 2 The Future

### What it means
Ask the AI to imagine the content has already been posted, several weeks have passed, and to analyse the results — what worked, what didn't, what should have been done differently. Then ask it to rewrite based on those imagined lessons.

### When Avalon should use it
- When a content idea is "good enough" but something feels off
- Before posting a series of carousels or Reels on a new destination
- When testing whether a CTA will actually convert
- After a post underperforms — to diagnose why in a structured way

### Example prompt

```
[BACK 2 THE FUTURE MODE]

Imagine this Reel has already been posted on @avalon.escapes. It's three weeks later. 

Tell me:
- Did it perform well? Specifically: was the reach good? Did the hook hold? Did comments or DMs come in?
- What did the audience respond to? What did they ignore?
- What fell flat? What was unclear, generic, or mismatched to the audience?
- What single change would have made the biggest difference?

Then: give me the rewritten version that would have performed better.

Post concept:
[DESCRIBE THE REEL CONCEPT + HOOK + CAPTION]
```

### How it helps content quality
- Forces a consequence-based perspective that pure ideation misses
- Makes failure modes visible before they happen
- Builds content intuition over time by practising retrospective thinking
- Often surfaces the single most important fix — not a laundry list

## 5. Style Clone

### What it means
Provide a writing sample or describe a specific voice, and ask the AI to match that style precisely — without copying exact words, phrases, or identity.

### When Avalon should use it
- When a caption needs to sound more like Rafa or Sofia specifically
- When rewriting reference creator content into Avalon's voice
- When testing whether a piece of content sounds authentic or formulaic
- When onboarding a collaborator or ghostwriter to Avalon's brand voice

### Example prompt

```
[STYLE CLONE MODE]

Study this example of Avalon's voice:

---
"No one prepares you for Fuvahmulah.

You go because you heard about the tiger sharks.
You stay because of everything else.

The silence underwater. The colour of the atoll at 6am. The feeling that you've arrived somewhere the world hasn't quite found yet.

Comment 'FUVA' and we'll send you our Maldives guide. 🌊"
---

This is the voice to match: premium but personal, short stanzas, emotionally precise, founder-speaking-not-agency-selling, specific sensory detail, quiet CTA at the end.

Now write a similar post for [NEW DESTINATION / NEW CONTENT IDEA] in the same voice. Do NOT copy any phrases from the example. Write as if Rafa or Sofia has been to this place and is speaking from personal experience.
```

### How it helps content quality
- Prevents the AI from defaulting to generic travel marketing language
- Ensures brand voice consistency across posts from different founders or collaborators
- Builds a reusable voice benchmark that gets sharper with use
- Catches the difference between "sounds like Avalon" and "is Avalon"

---

## Combining Methods

These methods can be stacked with GOAL prompts and with each other.

**Example: Devil's Advocate + Style Clone**
> First challenge my caption concept (Devil's Advocate), then rewrite the stronger version in Avalon's voice (Style Clone).

**Example: Client Lens + Back 2 The Future**
> Respond as our ideal client reading this post (Client Lens). Then imagine 3 weeks have passed — did they DM us? Did they save it? Did they scroll past? (Back 2 The Future)

**Example: Consult the Greats + GOAL**
> Evaluate this idea through expert lenses (Consult the Greats), then use the synthesised recommendation to fill in a full GOAL prompt.
